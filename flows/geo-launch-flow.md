# GEO Launch Flow

The buyer decides before the call. They read four confident sentences in an answer engine, and those
sentences name three companies as proof. If you are not one of the three, ranking will not save you:
a blue link sits on a page nobody scrolls. The engine quotes rather than ranks, and quoting rewards
content built to be lifted. This flow gets your name into the paragraph the buyer actually reads.
Three skills, in order, each gated by a checkpoint, taking you from a blind baseline to a measured
citation share and a recurring loop that defends it.

This is a growth flow, not a launch-week stunt. The output is not a spike in traffic. It is a
durable position in the rooms you cannot see, the model answers that mediate discovery before any
human reaches your site.

## The sequence

### Step 1 - Baseline your Share of Model (skill: measure-ai-native-pmf)
You cannot grow a number you never measured. Before you write a word, read where you stand. The
skill reads PMF across four lenses, and the one that matters here is Share of Model: how often the
engines reach for you when a buyer asks the question that should name you. That reading, taken cold,
is the baseline every later move is judged against.

**Produces:** a PMF read and, for this flow, a Share of Model baseline: your citation share on the
buyer queries that matter, against named competitors, before you have done anything.

**CHECKPOINT 1.** Is the baseline real or flattering? Confirm the query set reflects what buyers
actually ask, not the questions you already win. If the engines never name you on a core query,
record that as the headline, not a footnote. A baseline you cannot reproduce on a clean session is
not a baseline. Lock the query set here; you will reuse it unchanged for the rest of the flow and
the loop.

### Step 2 - Write content the engine will lift (skill: geo-content)
Now close the gaps the baseline exposed. The skill produces a GEO content brief and one piece built
to be quoted verbatim: a claim that stands on its own, a named mechanism, a quotable definition, a
number with its provenance attached. Not a vendor page that reads like every other vendor page. The
source the machine reaches for.

**Produces:** a GEO content brief and one published piece structured for extraction: standalone
claim, named mechanism, quotable definition, data with provenance.

**CHECKPOINT 2.** Test extractability before you call it done. Could the engine lift a paragraph and
have it stand alone, true and attributed, with no surrounding page? If the claim only makes sense
in context, or the number has no source, it will not be quoted. Every figure must survive a web
check, and in hard mode every health, food-safety, or regulatory line is confirmed with a qualified
professional before it ships. Rewrite until a paragraph survives being lifted.

### Step 3 - Measure the citation share (skill: share-of-model-audit)
Publishing is not proof. Run the locked query set across the engines and score it: who gets cited,
who gets named first, where you moved against the baseline. This is the read that tells you whether
the content earned its place in the answer or merely sits on your site.

**Produces:** a scored citation-share audit against the baseline, your first-mention share, and the
queries where a rival still owns the answer.

**CHECKPOINT 3.** Did citation share move on the queries you targeted, computed from the scored
answer sheet rather than from vibes? Every cited or not-cited cell must trace to a real engine
answer on a clean session. If the share did not move, the content was not extractable enough, and
you loop back to geo-content rather than publishing more of the same. A held query you already won
is not progress; a query you took from a rival is.

## What the flow outputs

- **A Share of Model baseline** on a locked buyer-query set, against named competitors, taken before
  any move.
- **Extractable GEO content**: a brief and one published piece built to be quoted, every figure
  sourced.
- **A scored citation-share audit** against the baseline, with first-mention share and the queries
  still owned by a rival.

## Hand off to the loop

A single reading lies. The engines rephrase, a model version changes its citation behaviour
overnight, a competitor ships a pillar page, and your number moves for reasons no snapshot explains.
What tells you anything is the series. Hand this flow's locked query set and baseline to the
`weekly-share-of-model-review` loop, which runs the same set weekly, diffs against last week, flags
the gaps, and drafts a brief for each, so you find out a rival is being cited in the rooms you
cannot see while you can still respond. The flow gets you onto the board. The loop keeps you there.

When the loop produces its first real move, a citation gained, a rival overtaken, a query lost and
won back, run the `capture-learning` skill so the lesson lands in the knowledge base dated and
sourced.
