# AI-Native OS Principles

Durable truths about building an AI-native company, distilled to first principles. Theme-agnostic.
Imported as master context by `CLAUDE.md` / `AGENTS.md` / `GEMINI.md` / Cursor rules. Grown over
time by the `capture-learning` skill: append, never overwrite.

## Founding principles (seed set)

- **Remove the AI and it should break.** If your product still works with the intelligence taken
  out, you built a feature, not an AI-native company. The moat lives in what breaks. *(seed)*
- **When everyone can build fast, building fast stops being the advantage.** The cost of syntax has
  hit zero; the scarce thing is judgement: what to build, what to refuse, and how to architect it
  so it compounds. The founder is the conductor, not the typist. *(seed)*
- **Architecture before code.** AI hides the bill on cheap prototyping. Specify the system (brain,
  memory, planning, tools) in a persistent file before an agent touches the repo. *(seed)*
- **A demo is not evidence.** A working prototype proves nothing about demand or about safety. The
  gap between a demo and the evidence required to act on a human is where hard-mode startups die. *(seed)*
- **AI supercharges confirmation bias.** Ask a model to justify your idea and it will, convincingly.
  The only safe default is adversarial: make it try to refute you. *(seed)*
- **The moat is data and trust, not the model.** Everyone rents the same foundation model. The
  defensible asset is the proprietary data flywheel you spin up and the trust a regulated buyer
  extends only after you earn it. *(seed)*
- **Changing anything changes everything (CACE).** ML systems entangle; an isolated improvement is a
  fiction. Pay down the highest-interest architectural debt first: the kind that blocks scaling or
  data collection. *(seed)*
- **Human-on-the-loop in sensitive domains.** Agents execute; humans set goals and review outcomes.
  In health, food-safety, finance, or anything irreversible, ship deterministic fallbacks before you
  ship autonomy. *(seed)*
- **Decouple revenue from headcount, deliberately.** The "One-Ten-Hundred" shape, a handful of
  senior people orchestrating agent networks, is a design choice, not an accident. Design the
  agent-centric org from day one. *(seed)*
- **Research before asserting.** A checkable claim (market size, regulatory timeline, benchmark) is
  either verified or labelled an assumption, never stated as fact on a guess. *(seed)*

## The Operator's Creed (generalised, post-hype)

Stated in sector-neutral form, the priorities that keep an AI-native company alive once the
excitement fades:

- **Evidence before story.** A great narrative on a hallucinating system still dies. *(seed)*
- **Trust before scale.** In regulated domains, scaling an unsafe agent multiplies the liability. *(seed)*
- **Closed loops before features.** A loop that learns beats a feature that impresses. *(seed)*
- **Proprietary data before generic intelligence.** What the horizontal giant can't reach is your edge. *(seed)*
- **Deterministic fallbacks before autonomy.** Earn the right to remove the human, one eval at a time. *(seed)*
