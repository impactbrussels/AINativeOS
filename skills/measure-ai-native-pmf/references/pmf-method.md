# Measure AI-Native PMF: The Method

The full read on whether you have product-market fit, not AI novelty. Four lenses, run in order.
Behaviour and money outrank anything the founder believes. The first purchase measures curiosity.
The fifth measures belief. This method reads the gap between them.

## The four lenses

| Lens | The question it answers | The bar |
|------|-------------------------|---------|
| 1. The 40% rule | Do active users depend on you? | More than 40% answer "very disappointed". |
| 2. Remove-the-AI | Is the fit native or borrowed? | What they pay to keep must break without your data. |
| 3. Share of Model | Are you visible where buying now starts? | Cited more than rivals across the assistants, trend rising. |
| 4. The second bite | Does trial become habit? | Retention flattens into a floor, reorders without a coupon. |

## Lens 1: the Sean Ellis 40% rule

Survey active users, never the sign-up list. One question: "How would you feel if you could no longer
use [product]?" Three options: very disappointed, somewhat disappointed, not disappointed. The bar is
the standard formulation: more than 40 percent answering "very disappointed" signals fit (Sean Ellis
test, verified in handbook SOURCES.md). It measures dependence, not enthusiasm. A five-star rating
from someone who never returns is not fit. Under 40 percent means sharpen the segment or the product,
do not raise.

## Lens 2: the Remove-the-AI test as a PMF lens

Run the test on the fit, not the stack. Ask what repeat buyers are actually paying to keep. If the
product still does its job once the model is gone, you measured demand for a feature anyone can rent,
and your retention is borrowed. The part they are loyal to has to be the part that breaks without your
proprietary data. Native retention compounds; borrowed retention is rented from whoever they switch
to next.

## Lens 3: Share of Model

Run 20 to 50 real buyer-intent queries across ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity. For each answer, record
one of three: cited me, cited a rival, cited neither. That ratio is the new top of the funnel, the
visibility in the channel where considered buying now starts. Watch the trend across weeks, not the
snapshot. Name the one competitor cited most, and write a one-line hypothesis why.

## What retention and cohorts actually prove

| Signal | What it proves | What it does not prove |
|--------|----------------|------------------------|
| Total signups | Distribution worked once. | That anyone came back. |
| Trial spike, flat repeat | You can launch. | You have a business. |
| Cohort survives months later | Habit formed, value is real. | Nothing, this is the signal. |
| Reorder without a coupon | Belief, not a discount. | Nothing, this is the signal. |
| A waitlist | Curiosity with good copy. | Demand. Never raise on it. |

A spike in trial with flat repeat is a warning dressed as a win. Trial is the cost of finding out;
habit is the business.

## Separating AI novelty from real demand

AI made the vanity lie cheaper: a model writes the onboarding, a launch post lands, ten thousand
people try it in a week, the graph looks like fit. It is curiosity with good distribution. Novelty
spikes and decays; demand persists and reorders. The tell is the second bite. If the May cohort still
uses what the January cohort tried, the AI was the hook and the product is the reason. If only the top
of the curve survives, you sold the novelty.

## Worked PMF read: a contract-review assistant

A founder claims fit: 12,000 signups, a viral launch, glowing reviews.

| Lens | Reading | Verdict |
|------|---------|---------|
| 40% rule | Surveyed 300 active users (not the 12,000): 31% "very disappointed". | Under bar. Enthusiasm, not dependence. |
| Remove-the-AI | They pay to keep the clause library built from their own past contracts. That breaks without the data. | Native, not borrowed. |
| Share of Model | 40 buyer queries: cited 6 times, top rival cited 19. | Losing the channel. Rival owns the trend. |
| Second bite | January cohort 22% active in May; reorders need a discount nudge. | Trial spike, soft floor. |

**Verdict:** not yet PMF. One native asset (the clause library) is real, but dependence is below the
bar and the channel is lost. The competitor beating Share of Model publishes buyer guides the
assistants cite. **Next action:** narrow to the segment that scored "very disappointed", earn the
fifth purchase there, then contest Share of Model.

## Anti-patterns and their fixes

| Anti-pattern | What it looks like | Fix |
|--------------|--------------------|-----|
| Vanity metrics | Total users and traffic on the deck; both climb whether or not anyone returned. | Report active-user dependence and cohort retention, not totals. |
| Demo-as-evidence | "Users love it" from a live demo or a five-star review. | A demo is not evidence. Run the 40% survey on active users. |
| Counting signups not retention | 12,000 signups headlined; the survival curve hidden. | Lead with the cohort floor and the reorder rate. |
| Raising on a pilot or waitlist | Interest reframed as fit to time a raise. | Pilots and waitlists measure interest. Do not raise on them. |
| Unverified hype number | A figure quoted that cannot survive a web check. | If it fails ten minutes of independent verification, it does not ship. |

```
AI-NATIVE PMF READ: [PRODUCT]
ICP: [ICP]   Domain: [DOMAIN]   Date: [DATE]

LENS 1 - 40% RULE (active users only, not signups)
  Surveyed: [N] active users.  "Very disappointed": [__]%   Bar: >40%   Verdict: pass / fail

LENS 2 - REMOVE-THE-AI
  Repeat buyers pay to keep: ____________________
  Does it break without your data? yes = native / no = borrowed

LENS 3 - SHARE OF MODEL
  [20-50] queries across ChatGPT / Claude / Perplexity.
  Cited me [_] | rival [_] | neither [_].  Top rival: ________  Hypothesis why: ________
  Trend: rising / flat / falling

LENS 4 - SECOND BITE
  Cohort: [month] survived to [month] at [__]%.  Reorder without coupon? yes / no

VERDICT: PMF / not yet.  Native retention? yes / no
NEXT ACTION (to earn the fifth purchase): ____________________
```
