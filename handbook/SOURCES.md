# Sources & Verification

This handbook draws on a synthesis of secondary AI-startup literature (press coverage, company
communications, funding announcements, sector market-maps) plus the author's own experience in EU
food-biotech and regulatory innovation. In the chapter text, any company-specific figure, a
valuation, revenue number, headcount, percentage, or dollar amount, carries a `[claimed in source]`
tag, because it originates in third-party reporting that may be stale, marketing-shaped, or simply
wrong. This file records the verification status of those claims as of June 2026 so a reader can weigh
the **pattern** over the **digits**, and confirm anything before citing it. Be aware that several of
the named companies in the handbook could **not** be independently verified from reputable sources;
where that is the case they are marked below and most likely originate in the secondary source
synthesis: treat them as illustrative, not as documented facts.

## Legend

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| ✅ | **Verified**: corroborated by a reputable, independent source. |
| ◐ | **Partially verified**: the entity is real and broadly as described, but a specific figure (valuation, revenue, percentage) is unconfirmed, contested, or self-reported. |
| ⚠ | **Claimed in source, unverified**: no credible independent source found; likely from the source synthesis. Treat as illustrative. |
| ✎ | **Illustrative / model**: a pattern, framework, or composite, not a literal claim about a single named entity. |

## How figures are handled

- **`[claimed in source]`** in the chapter text means: a public source asserts this; it has not been independently verified here. Cross-check against a primary source before repeating it.
- Treat any specific figure as **illustrative of a pattern**, never as a benchmark to plan or invest against. Valuations, ARR, and adoption percentages move quickly and are often self-reported.
- Where a named entity is marked **⚠** above, no credible independent source was found; it most likely originates in the secondary AI-startup synthesis and should be read as a model/illustration rather than a documented case.
- The handbook's first principle is **research before asserting**. This file exists to hold the book to its own standard: an unverified claim is labelled as such rather than restated as fact. If you can verify or correct any claim with a primary source, open a PR: see [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md).
