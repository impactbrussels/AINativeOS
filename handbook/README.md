# Load-Bearing

### Building AI-Native Companies in Hard-Mode Sectors

The handbook of [AI-Native OS](../README.md), by Adam M. Adamek (Impact Brussels ASBL).

The method, in depth: ~33,800 words on building a company where intelligence is the core, not the
garnish. Written for founders in hard-mode sectors (food, health, deeptech), including non-technical
ones.

> **The test the whole book is built around, Remove-the-AI:** take the AI out of your product. If it
> still works, you built an AI feature, not an AI-native company. Read [Chapter 00](00-introduction.md)
> first; it installs the test and shows you how to use the rest.

## How to read it

Each chapter is self-contained: it opens with the problem, gives you the core concepts, shows 2–3
real companies, hands you the exact AI workflows (which tool, which prompt), and ends with a "Your
turn" exercise and the AI-Native OS skill it spawns. Read it cover to cover, or jump to your stage.
Terms link into the [Dictionary](../dictionary/). Every named figure is logged in [SOURCES.md](SOURCES.md).

## A note on sources & honesty

The repo's first principle is *research before asserting*. Many figures in the source literature are
secondary and hard to verify; throughout the Handbook, specific numbers are tagged `[claimed in
source]` and the **pattern** is foregrounded over the number. [SOURCES.md](SOURCES.md) records each
claim and its verification status. Confirm any figure before you cite it.
